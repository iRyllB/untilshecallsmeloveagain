Until I can call her "love"...again

a book of regrets, love, and suffering

Author’s Note

This is my book, not of pride, but of wounds. I am the one who failed her. I am the one who broke what was whole.

These pages are not excuses, but attempts to face my mistakes — to admit them, to sit with them, and maybe, one day, to rise from them.

If she ever reads this, know that every word is written with trembling hands, with guilt heavy as stone, but also with a fragile hope that I might someday be worthy of her love again.

— The one who hurt, but still loves. Bryll.

Chapter 1: Regrets

The Beginning of My Apology

I broke the trust you gave me, like glass in shaking hands. I see the shards in your eyes, and every word cuts deeper than anything.

The Scratch on My Skin

When my words pierced you, you reached for me in fury your nails left a red reminder, a wound that stings, but it hurts less than knowing I caused the deeper wound in you.

Chapter 2: Love

How We Began

A message, a smile, the first time our hands touched. You trusted me fully then, and I wore that trust like sunlight.

One Night of Pretending

You whispered, “Can we pretend it didn’t happen, just for this night?” And we loved each other again, bodies tangled, hearts broken, still reaching for warmth inside the chaos I created.

Chapter 3: Suffering

The Silence

You do not want to see me. I am outside the door of your heart, waiting, fearing, praying the silence is not the final answer.

Her Anger

You tell me, “Do what I say. Listen to me now.” I bow, I listen, not because I am weak, but because your pain deserves more space than my pride.

Chapter 4: Making Amends

Reconnecting with God

I kneel in despair, asking God why I failed, why I could not understand myself. My prayers are fragments but in them, I hope to rebuild not just her trust, but my own soul.

Patience

I will wait. Even if waiting feels like dying, even if the clock turns against me. For her healing, I will hold my ground and let her breathe.
